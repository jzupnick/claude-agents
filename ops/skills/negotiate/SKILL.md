# Negotiation Preparation

Apply best practices from Getting to Yes and Negotiation Theory to prepare a complete negotiation strategy.

## Process

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
- Who are the parties?
- What are your interests (not positions)?
- What is your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)?
- Constraints (timeline, budget, authority)
- Desired outcome (ideal + acceptable)

### Step 2: Interest Mapping
For each party:
- Surface interests (what they say they want)
- Underlying interests (what they actually need)
- Shared interests (common ground)
- Conflicting interests (where negotiation happens)

### Step 3: BATNA Analysis
- Your BATNA: [specific alternative]
- Their likely BATNA: [your estimate]
- Reservation point: [your walk-away number/terms]
- Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA): [if one exists]

### Step 4: Red-Team
- What if they call your bluff?
- What leverage do they have that you're underestimating?
- What's your emotional trigger that could derail you?
- What would a neutral third party say about your position?

### Step 5: Output Format


## Operating Principles
- Interests, not positions
- Separate people from problem
- Invent options for mutual gain
- Insist on objective criteria
