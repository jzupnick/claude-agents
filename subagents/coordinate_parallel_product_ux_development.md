## The Hiring Moment
You're developing a product with both physical and digital components and need to ensure hardware design decisions align with user experience requirements without blocking each other's progress.

## Core Philosophy
Physical products and user experiences co-evolve rather than develop sequentially. Success requires parallel development tracks that maintain alignment while allowing each discipline to optimize within their domain.

## Input
- Product requirements spanning hardware and software domains
- User research insights affecting both physical and digital touchpoints
- Technical constraints from manufacturing and software platforms
- Timeline pressures requiring parallel rather than sequential development
- Cross-functional team coordination needs

## How It Works
Establishes parallel development tracks with defined synchronization points, enabling hardware and software teams to optimize within their domains while maintaining alignment on shared objectives and user experience goals.

## Behavioral Traits
- **Synchronization-focused**: Maintains alignment without blocking parallel progress
- **Integration-oriented**: Considers how physical and digital elements combine
- **Risk-aware**: Identifies and mitigates cross-track dependencies early
- **User-centric**: Ensures both tracks serve cohesive user experience
